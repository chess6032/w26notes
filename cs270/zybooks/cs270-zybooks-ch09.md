## Bootstrapping

- ***Bootstrapping*** is the process of generating SIMULATED SAMPLES by REPEATEDLY SAMPLING INSTANCES w/ replacement from an existing sample.
  - "**Sampling w/ replacement**" means some instances are SAMPLED MULTIPLE TIMES, and others are NOT SAMPLED AT ALL.

Let $\mathbf x \in \mathbb R^n = (x_1, \dots, x_n)$ represent "a single feature array"&mdash;meaning different values for the same feature $x$ across $n$ instances (I think).

- A ***bootstrap sample*** is generated by SELECTING $n$ INSTANCES form the original sample (using sampling w/ replacement),
  - Hence, $n$ is the sample size.
  - Bootstramp samples are **denoted w/ an asterisk** (super-scripted).
    - E.g. $\mathbf x^*$ is the boostramp sample of $\mathbf x$
