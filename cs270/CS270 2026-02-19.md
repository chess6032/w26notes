## What if data isn't linear separable??

**Transform your data into a new vector space where it is linearly separable!!!!** (Woahhh that's so cool!)

You'll be adding dimensions by generating a new "feature" via a **function that operates on the data**. The tricky part is making sure it's a valid function.

### Ex.

Quadric transformation: $(x_1, x_2) \rightarrow (x_1, x_2, x_1^2, x_2^2, x_1x_2)$ 

### Kernel trick

SVM's linear classifier relies on an inner product between vectors (dot prod): $k(\vec x_i, \vec x_j) = \vec x_i \cdot \vec x_j = \vec {x_i}^T \vec x_j$

If every datapoint is transformed `phi: \vec x , the inner product becomes:

```math
K(\vec x_i , \vec x_j) = \phi (x_i) ^T \phi (x_j)
```

We call $\phi$ the "kernel function".

<!-- The important thing is that w/ the kernel function we don't have to operate within the higher dimension; we can operate within the space our data is in. -->

The kernel function allows us to have a short-hand way of representing these transformations: defining the dot product of $\mathbf x_i, \mathbf x_j$ into a higher dimensional space.
